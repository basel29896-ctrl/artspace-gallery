Concept & working prototype

Art is sold in rooms, not in grids.

ArtSpace is a subscription platform for artists to show and sell original work. Instead of another thumbnail grid, the collection hangs in a real 3D gallery room you walk with your scroll wheel — and before anyone buys, they can hang the piece on their own wall, in true perspective.

View the code How it's built

The problem

Online, every artwork is reduced to the same rectangle at the same size on the same white background. Scale disappears. Presence disappears. A 2 metre canvas and a postcard look identical, and the single question every buyer actually has — would this work in my room? — goes unanswered.

For artists

A room of their own, not a marketplace listing. Originals never leave private storage; only watermarked, downscaled renditions are ever served.

For buyers

Walk the collection, then put the piece on your own wall at the right size and angle before contacting anyone.

For the platform

Subscription tiers for artists rather than a commission cut, so the incentive is to make artists look good, not to tax every sale.

A gallery room, not a grid

The ten most-loved works are hung across three walls, each with its own spotlight. Scrolling sweeps the camera from the west wall through the north wall to the east — the camera sits at the centre of the room and looks outward, so work is read head-on rather than in the periphery.

Click to approach

Selecting a work eases the camera to a fixed viewpoint in front of it and opens a wall label — title, artist, medium, description, likes.

The room degrades to a static editorial grid when WebGL is unavailable, and for anyone who has asked their system for reduced motion. An orbiting camera is exactly the kind of vestibular trigger that setting exists for.

View in your space

Upload a photo of your room, then drag the artwork's four corners to match the angle of your wall. Choose a frame, tune brightness and warmth to your light, and export a watermarked preview.

Why this was the hard part. Independent corner dragging is a projective transform, and canvas 2D only does affine — no matrix can express it. The artwork is warped by solving the homography from the four corners, then drawing 512 subdivided triangles, each with its own affine approximation. Verified to 1.15 × 10-13 px of vertex error.

Protecting the work

Artists are handing over their inventory. The pipeline is built so the original file is never the thing on the internet.

Original uploadprivate bucket, never public, no client-reachable URL
Served renditionmax 1600px, tiled diagonal watermark, EXIF stripped
Row-level securityenforced in Postgres on every table
Buyer PIIinquiries readable only by the receiving artist

Canvas rendering and a disabled context menu are also in place, but they are deterrence, not protection — they do not stop a screenshot. The watermark and the never-serve-originals rule are the real safeguards, and that distinction is documented in the code rather than glossed over.

The rest of it

Artist profiles & studio

Each artist gets a public room with a masonry hang. Behind the login: drag-and-drop upload, inline editing, buyer inquiries with status tracking, and per-work views, likes, and enquiry counts.

Reads on a phone

The artwork page, profiles, and directory are responsive; the 3D room falls back to the editorial grid where it should.

Honest status

This is a working prototype, not a shipped product. The full interface, the 3D room, the perspective editor, the watermark pipeline, auth, and the artist studio are built and exercised end-to-end in a browser. Stripe billing is not implemented, and everything requiring a live database runs against fixture data until a Supabase project is connected. The screenshots on this page use generated placeholder artwork.
